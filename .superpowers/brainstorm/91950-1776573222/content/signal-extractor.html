Signal Extractor — revised methodology

Expanded from 5 to 8 methods. Each targets a different way the narrator conceals evidence.

EXHAUST — Signal Extractor Engine
1 — ABSENCE
What topics never appear across all sources?
A subject who never mentions money in 200 conversations has told you something. Silence is data.
2 — CONTRADICTION
Where does past-self disagree with present-self without noticing?
Same topic, different position, no acknowledgment of the shift. The narrator didn't update the story.
3 — FREQUENCY VS SALIENCE
What recurs constantly but isn't felt as central?
Bunnings 3.4×/month, never mentioned in conversation. The gap between what occupies you and what you claim occupies you.
4 — TONE SHIFTS
Same topic, different emotional register across time or context?
How you discuss money with family vs. with work contacts. The narrator calibrates tone by audience.
5 — ABANDONED THREADS
What was started and dropped without explanation?
Subscriptions cancelled within 30 days. Projects with one commit. The gap between intention and follow-through.
6 — CROSS-STREAM CONVERGENCE ★ new
What do independent sources confirm or contradict about each other?
When bank data, iMessage patterns, and conversation history independently point to the same thing — high confidence signal. When they conflict — that conflict is the finding.
7 — LINGUISTIC DISTANCING ★ new
Where does language pull away from direct ownership?
Passive voice, third-person self-reference, heavy hedging. Forensic linguistics: distancing language around specific topics reveals psychological avoidance.
8 — BASELINE ANOMALIES ★ new
Where does behaviour deviate from its own established pattern?
Not "is this unusual in general" but "is this unusual for you." Spike in late-night purchases. Sudden silence in a regular contact. Deviation from personal baseline is the signal.
