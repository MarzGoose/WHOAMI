### Digital exhaust as narrator-bypass channel

Personal data exports (Claude conversations.json, Google Takeout, bank CSVs, iMessage, browser history, Apple Health) constitute pre-narrator raw material. Unlike self-report or even behavioural interviews, these sources were generated without the narrator curating them for a present-tense audience. The design principle that follows: questions answerable from self-report are narrator-inside; questions answerable only from data bypass the narrator. Five productive question types identified — absence (what topics never appear), contradiction (where past-self and present-self disagree without noticing), frequency-vs-salience gaps (what recurs constantly but isn't felt as central), tone shifts (same topic, different emotional register across time), and abandoned threads (what was started and dropped).

**Source**: 19 Apr chat on downloading Claude chat history and productising the aggregation pattern.

**Verbatim**:
> Questions that can be answered from self-report are already inside the narrator. Questions answered by *data* — frequency, timestamps, what's absent, what's contradicted across two chats six weeks apart — bypass it.

**Why it matters**: Provides the question-design filter for WHOAMI Stream B (behavioural data mining). Until now, interview protocols risked re-narration through the same narrator-filter WHOAMI was built to circumvent. This crystallises the rule: if a question can be answered without the data, it doesn't belong in Stream B. Also: the mirror caveat — physical mirrors are accurate and people still see what they want; data mirrors will be the same. Receptivity is brought by the subject, not engineered by the tool. Relevant safeguard for evaluating WHOAMI output later.

### EXHAUST — product pitch candidates

Working name: **EXHAUST** (digital exhaust = pre-narrator behavioural data generated without curation)

Pitch directions captured 2026-04-19, pending refinement:

- **Power framing (preferred):** "Empires have been built with your data. Let it now build yours."
- **Reclamation:** "Google already knows. Your bank already knows. Now you can know too."
- **Accountability:** "Big tech has been reading your exhaust for years. Time you did."
- **Blunt:** "The data that built their billion-dollar profiles of you — pointed back at yourself."

**Why the power framing works**: reframes from "confront yourself" to "take back what's already yours" — psychologically softer entry point to a confronting product. Also doubles as the narrator-bypass explanation without using that language.
